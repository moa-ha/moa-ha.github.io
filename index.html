Leadership responsibilities of a manager

One of the most obvious roles of a people manager is providing leadership for the employees they oversee. Being a good leader is a complex task, and it’s critical the professionals in these roles set a positive example. For example, a people manager shouldn’t expect others to be punctual when they are consistently arriving late to meetings themselves. Instead, they should meet organizational expectations and inspire their direct reports to perform at their best. Good leaders must also be effective communicators. You can’t guide others successfully if they don’t understand your expectations. The best leaders can communicate clearly, concisely and respectfully. Additionally, managers are also often responsible for delegation. The manager can’t take on all the responsibilities of the staff, so it’s critical they can identify who is best equipped for each duty and delegate tasks appropriately.
